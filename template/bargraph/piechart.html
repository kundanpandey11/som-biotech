Description

Utilizing deep learning techniques, we are focusing on the prediction of splicing events in DNA sequences. Splicing plays a crucial role in gene expression by removing non-coding introns and joining coding exons to form mature mRNA. By training a deep learning model on DNA sequences and corresponding splicing annotations, we aim to accurately identify splice sites. This prediction capability enables a deeper understanding of gene regulation, alternative splicing patterns, and their implications in disease mechanisms. Such insights can facilitate the discovery of potential therapeutic targets and aid in developing precise interventions for genetic disorders and personalized medicine applications.
